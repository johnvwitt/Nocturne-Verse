Mist on Wren Hollow

by Elias Thorn

Morning hush in lavender gray, A heron floats the foggy bay. Old fenceposts sigh with mossy breath— The hush of sleep, not quite of death. Pines whisper low, their branches bent, As if they keep the firmament. Soft hoofprints melt on dew-soaked grass, Where dreams and creatures slowly pass. A chapel bell — long rusted still — Rings faintly from the window sill. The wind, a monk in tattered blue, Recites what time forgot to do. Some stories rise like ash from loam, And hang above a buried home. The hollow breathes, it does not cry— It waits beneath a mist-washed sky.
