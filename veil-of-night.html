In the Veil of Night

by A. Everhart

The last of the sun bleeds over pine, and shadows gather beneath the hill. The hush between the stars begins— a silence ancient, and perfectly still. Lantern moths drift on air like breath, while crickets tune their minor hymns. An owl turns on its silent wing, guarding the dark where memory dims. A stream runs black with mirrored sky, its ripples tracing fallen lore. Somewhere a bell, forgotten, tolls— but no one keeps the hour anymore. The bones of trees stretch long and lean, grasping toward a sky once known. Each limb a script of loss and flame, each root a song in buried stone. Wolves do not howl—they listen now, as if the veil may whisper through. And I, a breath among the reeds, forget my shape and fade like dew.
